The truth is rarely pure and never simple. - Oscar Wilde

I am an American Studies masters student at the University of Alabama.

My research interests include literature, history, and culture with an emphasis on transatlantic studies, and gender and sexuality in adaptation.

I am a two-time recipient of the Louise B. Tate Memorial Endowed Scholarship for American Studies.

Past research projects include a web-based presentation on links between race and religion and a physical presentation about sexuality and agency in Robert Louis Stevenson's Strange Case of Dr. Jekyll and Mr. Hyde (for which I won first place in Fine Arts and Humanities at the University of Alabama Undergraduate Research and Creativity Conference in April 2022.

Ongoing research and digital projects include the web archive Thomas Andrews, Shipbuilder, exploring the architect of the Titanic through photos; a comparative analysis of Alexander Hamilton's life in history and as portrayed in Lin-Manuel Miranda's musical, and an exploration of American influence on the later works of Oscar Wilde.
