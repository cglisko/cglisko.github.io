The truth is rarely pure and never simple. - Oscar Wilde

I am an American Studies masters student at the University of Alabama.

My research interests include literature, history, and culture with an emphasis on transatlantic studies, and gender and sexuality in adaptation.

I am a two-time recipient of the Louise B. Tate Memorial Endowed Scholarship for American Studies.

Past research projects include a web-basede presentation about links between race and religion and a physical presentation about sexuality and agency in Robert Louis Stevenson's Strange Case of Dr. Jekyll and Mr. Hyde (for which I won first place for Fine Arts and Humanities at the University of Alabama Undergraduate Research and Creativity Conference in April 2022.

Ongoing research and digital projects include a web archive commemorating Thomas Andrews, shipbuilder of the Titanic, which can be viewed here; a comparative analysis of Alexander Hamilton's life in history and as portrayed in Lin-Manuel Miranda's musical, and an exploration of American influence on the later works of Oscar Wilde.
