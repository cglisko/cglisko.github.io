Castiel Gray Lisko

I am a Victorianist

I am a first year masters student at the University of Alabama in Tuscaloosa, working towards dual degrees in Religion in Culture and American Studies.

My research interests include literature, history, and culture with an emphasis on race, gender, and sexuality studies.

In spring of 2022, I was awarded first place for Fine Arts & Humanities at the University of Alabama Undergraduate Research and Creativity Conference for my presentation, entitled "'Henry Jekyll's Favorite': Sexuality and Agency in Robert Louis Stevenson's Strange Case of Dr. Jekyll and Mr. Hyde.

Ongoing research projects include an analysis of intersectionality between race and religion, an exploration of racial and queer identities in the X-Men film franchise, and no fewer than five projects focusing on Oscar Wilde's novel The Picture of Dorian Gray.
